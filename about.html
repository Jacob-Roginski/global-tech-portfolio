About Me

I'm always been fascinated by the details—whether it's the design of a website, the layout of a park, or the rhythm of cereal on my dorm room skateboard around MSU. That everyday curiosity fuels my love for exploration and helps me appreciate how both digital and physical environments shape the way we experience the world.

I chose to focus on computers and design because I believe digital spaces have the power to shape our lives. From websites to apps, I concentrate on the delicate balance of clarity, connection, and meaning. This past year especially, I've had the chance to dive into a lot of new experiences that pushed me to grow. From diving team challenges to both projects, every familiar challenge, each project helped me understand not only what makes a good product, but why it matters to the people who use it.

Along the way, I've built a strong foundation in product management skills—understanding user needs, breaking down problems, prioritizing features, and communicating ideas in a way that aligns with execution. I'm working at the intersection of design, technology, and people. Finding the balance between art and execution, it helps drive me toward a meaningful goal, helping someone in their day.

I'm excited to keep learning, exploring, and creating solutions that make everyday interactions—digital or physical—feel a little more intuitive.
